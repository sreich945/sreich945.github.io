Castle Mountain Trip Report –  07/02/2023

 

Planned Itinerary:

June 21, 2023 (Wednesday):

      • Leave early from Calgary
      • Park at Castle Mountain Fire Lookout Trailhead
      • Hike / Scramble / Climb to Castle Mountain Hut
      • Possible explore Goat Plateau

 

June 22, 2023 (Thursday):

      • Wake up early in Castle Mountain Hut
      • Traverse east across Goat Plateau to the base of the South East Face of Eisenhower Tower
      • Climb South East Face of Eisenhower Tower
      • Rappel off Eisenhower Tower
      • Descent off Eisenhower Tower and hike back down the Rockbound Lake trail
      • Hike / Hitchhike back to Fire Lookout Trail to get back to car

 

Report

We packed up all of our stuff on the Tuesday night and woke up early on Wednesday. Unfortunately the weather had not been good the last few days and there were reports of a lot of snow further up the valley between Lake Louise and Jasper. As we were driving into the mountains we couldn't see the much of the mountains past Canmore through the low cloud cover. We actually missed the Castle Junction turnoff and got to the trailhead around 9:30.

Hiking to the viewpoint was uneventful. There were a couple good lookout point. The view point has a concrete foundation where the tower was. We then tried to follow the ACC directions which were fairly detailed. After the view point we kept going at the same level (not uphill) where you come quickly to the a large gully that is crossed a little way uphill at some boulders. At this point we lost the trail due to the ground being covered by a lot of snow. We decided to put on our spikes and head directly up hill and find the start of the scrambling / climbing by walking east along the base of the cliff. We got the base of the cliff fairly quickly and started trying to find where we should go. We started up one easy looking way and I got to a ledge and belayed Eila up. I decided to go up again on lead on what was definitely 5th class terrain until I got to a tree that had a retreat sling on it. We decided to lower from here and try again. We got back to the ledge and decided to try going up again this time more climbers right (East) and got to small ledge with a tree. From here we could see down into the fourth class terrain as well as what looked like a cairn. Unfortunately it was getting late and it was still raining / snowing so we decided to bail. Unfortunately during bailing our rope got stuck and I had to climb back up and leave some tac and a locker to retreat more easily. After getting to the bottom of the cliff we hiked down and east just before the large gully. Right at the edge of the large gully is where we found the fourth class terrain that we should have been going up and the cairn that marks it. It turns out we were too far west from where we should have been. From here we found the climbers trail that leads back to the fire lookout. It turns out instead of hiking directly up the slope we should have continued at the same level as the boulders at the first gully crossing. After getting back to the lookout we continued back down to the car and stayed in Banff. The next day we hiked Yamnuska.
